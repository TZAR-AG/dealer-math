- Length: 50-62 chars optimal, 65 max, 70 hard ceiling (mobile truncation)
- Stack ≥4 of 5 psychology triggers: loss aversion · self-implication · specificity · identity activation · curiosity gap with stakes
- Banned words: `scam`, `rip-off`, `fraud`, `secret`, `hidden`, `cheating`, `trick`, `lying`, `crooked`, `crook`, `liar`
- No ALL CAPS words (except acronyms: AU, F&I, US, UK), no exclamation marks, max 1 question mark
- Default OMIT "Australia/Australian" — channel name + voice + visuals carry AU anchor
- INCLUDE AU only when: AU-specific tax/lender/consumer law topic, OR comparison where US assumption likely

**Word substitutions (defamation-safe library):**
- "extracted" (NOT "stolen")
- "added to" (NOT "snuck into")
- "loaded" (NOT "padded")
- "marked up" (legitimate term)
- "industry practice" (NOT "scam")
- "the way the system works" (NOT "rip-off")
- "by design" (NOT "deliberate")
- "structural" (NOT "rigged")
- "not disclosed" (NOT "hidden")
- "the math behind" (NOT "secret")
- "what they don't show you" (NOT "hidden")
- "the one [thing] dealers hope you won't ask" (NOT "trick")

## Search vs browse strategy

**Search-intent titles** (Mode C — reinforce mode):
- Long-tail keyword phrasing ("how does novated lease work in Australia")
- Front-load the keyword in first 5 words
- Titles 60-65 chars (more keyword runway)
- Pair with reinforcing thumbnails

**Browse-intent titles** (Mode A/B/D):
- Hook-first phrasing ("the one question dealers hope you won't ask")
- Front-load the punch in first 3-5 words
- Titles 50-58 chars (tight, punchy)
- Pair with curiosity-gap thumbnails

AUDM at 5 subs is browse-discovery dominated. Default to browse-intent titles. Use Mode C / search-intent on ~15% of uploads to plant long-tail SEO seeds.

---

## Beginner vs advanced audience

**Beginner-friendly titles:**
- Use "explained", "in 7 minutes", "what to know"
- Define jargon in title ("F&I = Finance and Insurance" if niche-new)
- Pair with high-clarity thumbnails (Mode C)

**Advanced/insider titles:**
- Use industry terms unmoderated ("F&I", "novated", "comparison rate")
- Tease specifics ("the $4,800 line")
- Pair with curiosity-gap thumbnails (Mode A/B)

AUDM splits roughly 60% advanced / 40% beginner. The advanced topics earn authority; the beginner topics earn search traffic.
