## Tier A — Faceless analytical (DIRECT AUDM analogs, study these hard)

### 1. PolyMatter — "Why Only China Can Make The iPhone" (1.2M subs)

**Layout:** vertical split frame — left third = aerial farmland (BEFORE), right two-thirds = aerial industrial complex (AFTER), satellite/drone perspective.
**Text overlay:** stacked left-aligned hierarchy — "WHY / **ONLY CHINA** / CAN MAKE / THE iPHONE" with "ONLY CHINA" in lime-green, rest in white DM-Sans-style.
**Annotation tags:** lime-green pill labels ("PACKAGING", "DORMITORIES", "FINAL ASSEMBLY") with thin lines pointing to specific buildings.
**Mini-badges:** black-pill "BEFORE" + white-pill "AFTER" stacked at the split seam.
**Color palette:** desaturated earth tones + lime-green accent + white text. **Zero red, yellow, orange.**
**Why it works:** every element earns its space. Split-frame IS the curiosity gap. Annotation tags make viewer feel like an analyst. Not a single shouting word.
**AUDM verdict:** 🟢 GOLD STANDARD. Steal: split-frame transformation + annotation-callout tags + tight typographic hierarchy + lime-green replaced with outback orange.

### 2. PolyMatter — channel pattern (5+ videos audited)

**Recurring pattern:** 1-2 word ALL-CAPS hero phrase + curiosity contextualizer (white above/below) + always one accent color (varies per topic but ONE not three) + always one subject-anchored visual (map, satellite, document, building, currency).
**AUDM verdict:** 🟢 STEAL. This is the system. Not a one-off — a repeatable template that scales across the catalog.

### 3. Wendover Productions / Half As Interesting (~5M, ~7M subs)

**Recurring pattern:** integrated text + visual where typography sits IN the scene — labels point to map regions, statistics annotate flight paths, currencies annotate trade routes. Thumbnail mirrors the in-video animation style.
**Hierarchy:** one big scene (map/aerial/diagram) + 1-3 short labels with thin pointer lines.
**Color:** low-saturation, navy/cream/sand most common. Premium, NOT clickbait.
**AUDM verdict:** 🟢🟢 STEAL THE MECHANIC. Label-with-pointer-line translates 1:1 to a contract page with annotation tags pointing to specific clauses — and we already use this exact aesthetic in the V1 7-Lines cheatsheet. **The cheatsheet IS our channel-brand visual. Our thumbnails should look like our cheatsheet.**

### 4. How Money Works (800K subs, $57K/mo AdSense — gold-standard faceless finance)

**Recurring pattern:** single-symbol macro composition (close-up of a $100 bill, a fragment of a Bloomberg chart, a stack of coins, a credit card) + 2-3 word hero text + one secondary contextualizer line.
**Color palette:** ALWAYS US-currency-green or grayscale chart + one accent. Predictable, ownable, brand-identifiable from feed.
**Why it works:** instantly readable category cue. No face needed. The channel's brand IS the visual style.
**AUDM verdict:** 🟢🟢 STEAL THE MECHANIC. A macro of a charcoal-paper contract with one orange-circled clause IS the AUDM equivalent of HMW's macro-currency-on-grey. **Build a recognisable visual identity that announces the niche before you read a word.**

## Tier B — US dealer-reveal (Lucky Lopez, CarEdge — psychological pattern study, NOT visual copy)

### 5. Lucky Lopez — "Why Dealerships Lie" (~600K views)

**Layout:** right-third face anchor — male, beard, mouth wide-open in shocked "O", eyes dilated, leaning toward camera. Red drop-shadow halo behind face.
**Background:** wide-angle showroom interior with red/yellow Ferraris, fluorescent overhead lighting.
**Foreground prop:** red octagonal STOP sign reading "DEALERSHIP" planted between viewer and showroom.
**Text:** "**LYING!**" — bottom-left, white sans-serif on red rectangular fill, 200pt+.
**Color palette:** red dominates (sign + halo + LYING fill + Ferraris) + black-and-white grayscale background.
**Mechanism:** stop-sign metaphor triggers lizard-brain "HALT", face triggers mirror-neurons, single accusation word is the click commitment. Likely 8-12% CTR cold traffic.
**AUDM verdict:** 🔴 ANTI-PATTERN. Defamation-edge ("LYING"), face-led, screaming exclamation. Do NOT replicate. **DO study the metaphor-prop concept** (stop sign) — could become a charcoal-orange CAUTION TAPE or "DOTTED LINE" graphic in our system.

### 6. Lucky Lopez — "New Dealership SCAM"

**Layout:** left-third face (shocked, hands-up "stop" gesture, Ferrari hoodie) + center-right red-stamp "SCAM" overlay on greyed-out US $50 bill backdrop + lower-right SUVs in 3D-render style + green up-arrow chart line.
**Text:** distressed red rubber-stamp "**SCAM**" — single word, hero element.
**Why it might work despite chaos:** single dominant focal element wins the eye, then face confirms emotion, then chart hints at "this affects you."
**AUDM verdict:** 🔴 ANTI-PATTERN (defamation language). 🟡 BORROW the rubber-stamp-on-document mechanic — a charcoal "DEALER COST" or "NOT NEGOTIABLE" stamp on cream contract is the AUDM-translated version.

### 7. CarEdge — "IT BEGINS! The Car Market CRASH Of 2024 / Inventory SPIKES 195%" (~390K views)

**Layout:** right-third face anchor (Ray Shefska, mid-speech, glasses) + left/center wide-angle dealership lot + giant red curved up-arrow sweeping diagonally bottom-left to top-center.
**Text:** "**+195%**" lime-yellow caps over arrow tip — single number, drop-shadowed.
**Color palette:** lime-yellow + red arrow + desaturated greys. High-contrast, three-color discipline.
**Mechanism:** **single number** is the click-pivot (specific, irrefutable, "of what?" curiosity), arrow gives kinetic direction, face authenticates "an industry guy is telling me this." Title's "IT BEGINS!" carries urgency so thumb stays (relatively) clean.
**AUDM verdict:** 🟢 TRANSLATABLE. Steal: **single-number-with-arrow** as hero element. Replace: face → document, lime-yellow → outback orange, red arrow → orange arrow. A "+$4,800" with charcoal arrow over a contract is the defensible AUDM equivalent.

### 8. CarEdge — Channel title-pattern audit (10 top videos)

**Recurring patterns:**
- "Top 10 WORST & BEST Cars to Buy Right Now" (binary verdict)
- "Chevy, Ford & RAM Are F#@%ed | Customers ARE ANGRY" (named-villain + angry)
- "CarMax IS DONE!" (death-of-X declaration)
- "Don't Buy a Used Car YET! Wait 60-90 Days to Buy!" (timing imperative)

**AUDM verdict:** 🔴 NAMED-BRAND + DEATH/CRASH framing is defamation-edge for a dealer-reveal channel. Don't. 🟢 The **timing-imperative** ("Wait 60-90 Days") is borrowable — clean "BEFORE YOU SIGN" or "THE NEXT 7 DAYS" framing.

### 9. AutoExpert AU / John Cadogan — "Tesla Cybertruck: a crash test death trap" (109K views)

**Layout:** centered crash-test Cybertruck mid-impact, full-frame + "DEATH TRAP" lime-green caps overlay top-left + small mascot/avatar bottom-left.
**Color:** lime-green text on grey crash facility + Cybertruck silver. Two-color discipline + mascot accent.
**Mechanism:** specific named target + binary verdict + crash imagery as proof. Mascot replaces face.
**AUDM verdict:** 🟢 STEAL the **mascot replaces face** mechanic — but Mac is already locked faceless and a hand silhouette + brand mark already serves this role. The **crash-imagery-as-evidence** structure translates: a contract photo with ONE clause circled IS our crash-test photo.

### 10. AutoExpert AU — Channel title-pattern audit (15 top videos)

**Recurring patterns:**
- "Top 10 Ways to Beat a Car Dealer" (numbered + adversarial — cornerstone format)
- "How to beat a car dealer in 2022 (SAVE THOUSANDS!)" (action + outcome)
- "One critical question most car buyers don't ask at the dealership" (singular curiosity gap)
- "Car dealer ambush tactics (and how to beat them)" (insider reveal + counter)
- "How to save thousands on a brand new car in Australia" (outcome + AU anchor)

**AUDM verdict:** 🟢 STEAL the "How to beat a [process]" + "The one question" + numbered-list patterns. 🔴 SKIP the "Tesla = death trap / living hell" inflammatory verdicts (defamation surface).

## Color analysis — where AUDM stands out

The analytical-finance YouTube feed is dominated by:
- **Red urgency** (Lopez, How Money Works clickbait variants, Cornelia)
- **Lime-yellow shock** (Lopez SCAM, CarEdge +195%, Cadogan DEATH TRAP)
- **Green money / chart-up** (HMW, CarEdge arrows)
- **Blue digital / corporate** (negotiation channels, Wendover navy variant)

**AUDM's outback orange `#D17A3D` + charcoal `#2B2B2B` + cream `#F5EFE6` palette is genuinely uncommon.** Closest neighbours: PolyMatter's olive-and-cream and Wendover's navy-and-cream — both faceless premium. **In a row of red/yellow/lime thumbnails, an orange-on-charcoal slate reads as the credible adult in the lineup.** That's the click-promise of the brand. Hold the discipline.

## V1 + V2 thumbnail rebuild specs (urgent — execute this week)

### V1 — "I Sold Cars for 10 Years — Never Answer This Question" (current CTR 1.7%)

**Diagnosis:** thumb says "NEVER ANSWER THIS ONE QUESTION" + tiny hand-on-contract = stacked-text-poster pattern WITHOUT supporting visual + text duplicates title verbatim. Wastes 50% click-juice. 5-line text density blocks foveal singularity.

**3 alternatives, ranked by predicted CTR lift:**

| Rank | Composition | Predicted CTR lift |
|---|---|---|
| **🥇 1st** | Template B (Number on Document): "**$0**" massive orange figure left-third + tight macro of contract clause + pen tip + tiny white tag "WHAT TO ANSWER" or "WORTH". Number is paradoxical — drives "$0 of what?" | +3-5pp (3.0-4.5% range) |
| **🥈 2nd** | Template A (Circled Clue): macro of contract clause with ONE orange hand-circle around the question line + 2-word "**DON'T ANSWER**" left-third white, 240pt DM Sans Bold | +2-3pp |
| **🥉 3rd** | Template D (Stamped Overlay): contract macro + orange `STAY SILENT` rubber-stamp at 15° + no other text | +2pp |

### V2 — "Why Every Australian Buyer Loses $4,800 in This One Room" (current CTR 0.5%)

**Diagnosis:** thumb says "$4,800 LOST" + "ONE ROOM" = title-duplicate disease, fragmented hierarchy (3 separate text blocks), document too dim to carry weight, the ONE element that could have been a forensic hero (the orange circle on contract) is microscopic.

**3 alternatives, ranked:**

| Rank | Composition | Predicted CTR lift |
|---|---|---|
| **🥇 1st** | Template C (Split-Frame Transition): left half cream-quote-sheet sticker with "$X,XXX" in DOF blur + orange circle (BEFORE/quoted), right half wider contract showing add-on lines stacked with orange highlight (AFTER/actual). Black "QUOTED" + orange "PAID" pill-tags at seam. **No further text** — title carries $4,800. | +4-6pp (4.5-6.5% range) |
| **🥈 2nd** | Template E (Annotated Cheatsheet): zoom on F&I-office line of 7-Lines cheatsheet + 2-3 orange pointer-tags with cream pill labels: "**ADD-ONS**" / "**~$1,200**" / "**~$2,400**" (sums to $4,800 without saying it) | +3-4pp |
| **🥉 3rd** | Template B (Number on Document): different number — show cost-base not loss. Big orange "**$390**" + tiny white "WHAT IT COST THEM" caption + cream contract macro. Title supplies $4,800; thumb supplies markup math. | +3-4pp |

**Key insight — the rule of non-redundancy:** title and thumbnail must answer DIFFERENT questions. AUDM has wasted half its click-juice for two videos straight. V3 onwards: this is the load-bearing rule.

## The 5 anti-patterns AUDM must NEVER use

1. **Shocked face / open-mouth / wide-eyes anchor** — Lucky Lopez / CarEdge formula. Faceless lock + premium-positioning lock kill this.
2. **Yellow / red / lime-green dominant palette** — outback orange is the ONLY accent. Yellow circles, red rectangles, neon-green text = brand-violation.
3. **Defamation-edge stamp/text** — "SCAM", "FRAUD", "LYING", "RIP-OFF", "EXPOSED", "TRICKED". Voice-rule + legal-risk territory.
4. **Title-duplicate text overlay** — V1 + V2 disease. Thumbnail text must add a *second* curiosity layer, never restate.
5. **All-caps screaming with exclamation marks** — "DEATH TRAP!" / "LYING!" / "+195%" with shout punctuation. Calm authority delivers the same impact via clean assertion.

3. **Five-color chaos** (Cornelia collabs) — calm authority requires three-color discipline
