## Anchor decisions (the 5 answers)

### Q1 — The narrow problem (PDF spine)
**Dealer delivery is the spine.** Universal applicability (100% of new car buyers vs 30-70% for other moves), earliest in the buyer journey (appears on contract before financing), cleanest fix to teach.

**Sharper fix than current PDF:**
- Cap mass-market dealer delivery at **$500-$1,000**
- Cap luxury at **$1,500-$2,000**
- Anything above the cap = dealer margin disguised as cost
- Tactic: tell the dealer the cap is your max, walk if they refuse

### Q2 — The moat / value gap (paid product)
**The Counter-Script Library** — Adrian's 10 years of objection-handling, reverse-engineered for buyers. For every objection the dealer throws, the exact word-track to flip it back. Includes Adrian's urgency-building tactics turned defensive.

This is the $29 product. The PDF reveals the LIBRARY EXISTS without giving its content.

### Q3 — Defamation calibration
Adrian's stance: not paranoid (industry knowledge is broadly corroborable, "industry-practice analysis" hedge protects).

Decision: write in **testimonial framing** as a writing technique, not (just) a legal hedge:
- "30% of PDIs are tick-box" → "PDI thoroughness varied dramatically"
- "Dealer delivery is markup" → "What they call dealer delivery is mostly margin"
- Voice gets stronger AND defamation surface lower. Twofer.

### Q4 — Give-the-WHAT test
Resolved: hybrid structure.
- **Page 3 (dealer delivery anchor):** Full WHAT + HOW. Reader saves $1.5-3K standalone. Trust handshake.
- **Pages 4-9 (other 6 moves):** Name the framework + reveal hidden mechanic + V2-V12 hook. Reader recognises move on showroom floor; needs video for the choreography to counter it.
- **Page 10 (moves not covered):** Just names. Pure curiosity gap.

The reader is satisfied + thirsty simultaneously. Hormozi's salty-pretzel rule honoured.

### Q5 — The moat sentry (opening anchor of $29 product)
**The 5-minute DISC profile reveal.** Within 5 min of buyer walking in, the salesperson has typed them (Dominant / Influence / Steady / Conscientious) and is running a calibrated script. The buyer doesn't know.

This is the **opening line of the paid product**, NOT in the PDF. The PDF only hints at its existence ("they had you typed before you sat down — there's a name for it").

## Writing rules locked

1. **Testimonial framing every line.** "In my experience" / "what I saw" / "the dealerships I worked at" — never authoritative-assertional.
2. **Mac, not Macca.** Customer-side rename locked 2026-05-01.
3. **Names of moves freely; scripts withheld.** "The Manager Lie" can be named in the PDF; the actual word-track lives in the $29 Counter-Script Library.
4. **Specific dollar ranges, not specific dollar figures.** "$500-$1,000" not "$847."
5. **No specific brand names** anywhere in the PDF (V11 video is the only place Mercedes / Honda are named, per locked brand-naming policy).
6. **Each line page ends with one specific V2-V12 hook by date.** Generic "subscribe" CTAs banned.
7. **Page 10 names 5+ frameworks not unpacked anywhere in the PDF.** Pure curiosity gap.
8. **Final CTA is a video-specific cliffhanger** — Hormozi's lead-magnet cardinal rule.
