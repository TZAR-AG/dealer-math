# YouTube Analytics Baselines for AUDM (Mac)

**Created:** 2026-05-01
**Mission:** AU Dealer Math — @audealermath
**Use:** Reference for interpreting V1 launch metrics (Sun 3 May 2026, 19:00 AWST) at Day 7 / Day 30 / Day 90 review checkpoints.
**Niche frame:** AU consumer finance / education sub-niche (dealer math), faceless ex-dealer persona, long-form + Shorts pairing.

> Use this doc as the only baseline reference when reading AUDM analytics. Don't trust gut feel ("we got 200 views, is that good?") — score every metric against the bands below.

---

## 1. Week 1 health signals (Day 1–7)

These are the numbers that tell us "the algorithm has noticed this channel" vs "we're still in the cold-start sandbox."

### Long-form V1 (8-15 min Mac dealer-reveal video)

| Metric | "This is working" | "Mixed signal" | "This isn't working" |
|---|---|---|---|
| Views in first 48h | 150–500 | 50–149 | <50 |
| Views by Day 7 | 400–1,200 | 150–399 | <150 |
| Average view duration (AVD %) | ≥45% | 35–44% | <35% |
| Average view duration (absolute) | ≥4:00 on a 9-min video | 3:00–3:59 | <3:00 |
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 6–10% | 4–5.9% | <4% |
| Watch time (hours, Day 7) | ≥30 hrs | 10–29 hrs | <10 hrs |
| Subscribers gained (Day 7) | 15–50 | 5–14 | <5 |
| Comments per 100 views | ≥0.8 | 0.3–0.7 | <0.3 |
| Return viewer rate (Day 7) | n/a (too early) | n/a | n/a |

**Why the bands:** Educational/finance channels in the new-channel sandbox sit at 150–500 views in the first 48h driven by search + Suggested traffic. CTR 6–10% is the sub-1K-subs band; below 4% means thumbnail/title is overpromising or undercurious. AVD ≥45% on long-form is what tells YouTube "this is worth recommending"; below 35% triggers the Quality CTR penalty — algo demotes regardless of click rate.

### Shorts (5 launching alongside V1)

| Metric | "This is working" | "Mixed signal" | "This isn't working" |
|---|---|---|---|
| Views per Short, 48h | 200–2,000 | 50–199 | <50 |
| Best-of-5 Short, 48h | ≥3,000 | 1,000–2,999 | <1,000 |
| Like-to-view ratio | ≥4% | 2–3.9% | <2% |
| Viewed-vs-swiped (VVSA) | ≥70% | 60–69% | <60% |
| Subs gained from Shorts | 5–25 across all 5 | 1–4 | 0 |

**Why:** Shorts are evaluated meritocratically (each Short on its own merits, channel authority barely matters), so variance is huge — a single Short hitting 5K while four hit 50 is normal. Like-to-view <2% means it didn't connect emotionally. VVSA <60% means the first 1–2 seconds aren't holding the swipe-finger.

**Day 7 verdict rule:** If long-form V1 hits ≥400 views with ≥45% AVD AND at least one Short clears 1,000 views, the channel is on the healthy track. If V1 is <150 views AND every Short is <100, we're not getting algo distribution — pivot lever needed (see § 4).

## 2. Month 1 health signals (Day 8–30)

By Day 30 we should have ~5 long-form videos shipped (per V1-V12 calendar) plus ~15-20 Shorts. The trajectory matters more than any single video.

| Metric | Healthy trajectory | Flatline trajectory |
|---|---|---|
| Cumulative views (long-form) | 3,000–8,000 | <2,000 |
| Cumulative views (Shorts) | 8,000–25,000 | <3,000 |
| Subscribers (Day 30) | 100–300 | <50 |
| Best-performing video views | ≥1,500 | <500 |
| AVD on the median video (long-form) | 40–55% | <35% |
| CTR trending direction | Stable or rising 4–10% | Falling below 4% |
| Return viewer rate | ≥8% | <3% |
| Search traffic % of total views | Trending toward 10% | <5% with no growth |
| Comments per 100 views (median) | ≥0.5 | <0.2 |
| Watch time (hours, cumulative) | ≥150 hrs | <60 hrs |

**Why these bands:** Average new YouTube channel takes ~15.5 months to 1K subs (vidIQ data); finance/education niches with valuable content compress that to 6–12 months. So 100–300 subs by Day 30 = on track for ≤12-month 1K. Search traffic should be *building* by Day 30 even if it's not at 10% yet — that's the SEO foundation paying back. Return viewer rate is the single strongest predictor of long-term viability — above 10% by end of Month 1 is excellent for a finance channel.

**Successful Month 1 trajectory:** views per video doubling roughly every 5–7 videos; one "breakout" video by V4 or V5 that 3-5×s the channel median; subs climbing in step jumps after each breakout, not flatline.

**Flatline trajectory:** every video lands within ±20% of the same view count; subs growing linearly in single digits per video; no breakout. This is the "channel isn't earning algo trust" pattern.

## 3. Month 3 health signals (Day 60–90)

Day 90 is the algorithmic inflection point. YouTube's profiling period is 90 days / 12–15 videos — by then YouTube has classified the channel and decided whether to feed it Suggested + Browse traffic or leave it in search-only mode.

| Metric | "Inflection passed" | "Still on probation" | "Algo not biting" |
|---|---|---|---|
| Subscribers (Day 90) | 500–1,500 | 200–499 | <200 |
| Cumulative views | 30,000–80,000 | 10,000–29,000 | <10,000 |
| Median views per video | 1,500–4,000 | 500–1,499 | <500 |
| Best video views | ≥10,000 | 3,000–9,999 | <3,000 |
| Browse + Suggested as % of traffic | 30–50% | 15–29% | <15% |
| Search as % of traffic | 15–30% | 30–50% (over-reliant) | <10% |
| AVD % (median) | 45–55% | 35–44% | <35% |
| CTR (median) | 5–9% | 4–4.9% | <4% |
| Return viewer rate | ≥15% | 8–14% | <8% |
| Watch time (hours, cumulative) | ≥600 hrs | 200–599 hrs | <200 hrs |

**Why these bands:** "Inflection passed" channels hit 1K subs around Month 4–6 from this base, then 10K around Month 12–18, then YPP-meaningful revenue. "Still on probation" channels can recover with a format pivot in Months 4–5. "Algo not biting" channels almost never recover without a hard reset of either niche, persona, or thumbnail strategy.

**Key signal at Day 90:** the Browse + Suggested mix. Channels stuck >50% search-traffic at Day 90 are being treated by YouTube as a long-tail SEO library, not a recommendable creator — that caps growth at "people actively googling dealer tactics," which is a tiny pool. We need Browse + Suggested ≥30% by Day 90 to unlock real scale.

## 4. Pivot triggers (the 7 thresholds that force a change)

These are the "if-then" rules — when a metric breaches the trigger, change one specific thing rather than panicking and changing everything.

1. **AVD <35% at Day 14 (any video) → re-cut the script structure.** Move the strongest reveal/payoff to under 1:30. Mac is teaching too long before the hook lands. Re-edit V3+; don't re-shoot. (Source: 40% retention is the algo deprioritisation floor; 35% is the leading indicator.)

2. **CTR <4% at Day 30 (median across all videos) → thumbnail strategy pivot.** Run the Document Forensics template on every V5+ video; retire any thumbnail without the orange-highlight-on-paper element. Keep titles unchanged for one cycle so the variable is isolated. (Source: 4% is the universal "you're underperforming" floor for educational content.)

3. **Return viewer rate <5% at Day 30 → voice/persona is not landing.** Mac as a character isn't earning the second-watch. Test one V6+ video with a tighter Mac POV (more "I was there in the dealership" anchoring) before declaring the persona broken. (Source: ≥10% is the strong band; <5% suggests viewers don't remember the channel.)

4. **Subscriber-to-view ratio <0.3% at Day 60 → CTAs are weak or value isn't sticky.** Educational benchmark is 1–3% conversion (subs ÷ views). If we're at 0.3%, the watch experience is fine but the subscribe ask is missing or the next-video promise is invisible. Add explicit "tomorrow's Mac dealer reveal" cards in last 30s of every video. (Source: educational content 1–3% conversion benchmark.)

5. **Best-video views <1,500 by Day 30 → format experiment required.** No breakout means the algo isn't surfacing any of our work in Browse + Suggested. Test ONE video in V8/V9 with a different format (interview-style, document-walkthrough, top-X-list) to break the pattern. Don't pivot every video — A/B against the locked Mac monologue format. (Source: healthy trajectory shows a 3–5× breakout by V4–V5.)

6. **Quality CTR mismatch (CTR ≥6% AND AVD <35%) on any video → kill that title/thumbnail combo.** The 2026 algo specifically demotes "high-CTR low-retention" videos as overpromise patterns. Update title within 7 days of post; never repeat that hook formula. (Source: Quality CTR is a 2026 algo update — high click + low watch is now actively penalised.)

7. **Search traffic >50% of total at Day 90 → break out of the SEO sandbox.** We're being classified as a long-tail SEO library, not a recommendable creator. Force a Suggested-feed test: post a video deliberately optimised for *related-to-MoneySmart-and-Mr-Money-TV* Suggested placement (cite their angles, use complementary thumbnail style). One video, see if Suggested impressions move. (Source: <30% Browse + Suggested at Day 90 is the algo-ceiling pattern.)

## 5. Niche specifics — Australia + dealer math

### AU CPM advantage

- **AU finance niche RPM:** $14–$22 AUD per 1,000 monetised views — 88% of US rates, materially above global average ($4–$8 RPM).
- **AU finance niche CPM:** $20–$36 AUD.
- **For comparison:** AU education (general) is $4–$6.50 RPM. Finance-coded content earns 3–4× the general-education rate.
- **Implication:** AUDM doesn't need US scale to be revenue-meaningful. 50,000 AU monthly views ≈ $700–$1,100 AUD/month ad rev. 200,000 AU monthly views ≈ $2,800–$4,400 AUD/month — that's the realistic 12-month target if we hit the inflection.

### Dealer-math sub-niche specifics

- **Audience size is small but conversion-dense.** AU car-buyer search volume is ~10× smaller than US; we won't see 100K-view videos in Year 1. Realistic Year 1 ceiling on a single video: 25K–50K views for a strong topic, 5K–15K for a typical topic.
- **High advertiser bid density.** Dealer-math content sits at the intersection of automotive (auto-dealer + insurance ads) AND finance (loan + credit-card ads) — both above-average CPM categories. Expect AUDM RPM at the **upper end** of the AU finance band ($18–$22) once the audience is established.
- **Lead-magnet carry-through is the multiplier.** With an AU audience this targeted, the 7-Lines cheatsheet email capture matters more than ad RPM. A 5% email-capture rate on 50K cumulative views = 2,500 AU car-buyers in our Kit list — that's a paid product / partnership audience, not just an ad-revenue audience.
- **Defamation-safety constrains scale.** AUDM voice rules ("industry practice", not "scam") cap viral potential vs hostile-tone competitors. We trade Year 1 explosive growth for sustainability — accept slower trajectory than a US ranter would see.

### Comparable channels (calibration)

- **Mr Money TV (Peter Yong):** 153K subs, took 4–5 years, MY/AU/SE-Asia mix. Demonstrates the patient-build path for AU-region finance.
- **MoneySmart-style explainer channels:** typically 10–30K subs at Year 2, 50–100K by Year 4 in AU finance education.
- **AUDM realistic year 1 target:** 1.5K–5K subs by Day 365. Year 2 target: 10K–25K. The channel earns its way to scale; it doesn't shortcut.

## If you only remember three numbers

**45% / 4% / 10%.** At every review checkpoint (Day 7, 30, 90), the three numbers that matter most are: **45% average view duration** on long-form (below 35% = algo demotion zone), **4% CTR** as the floor for thumbnail/title health (below it = packaging problem), and **10% return viewer rate** as the leading indicator of long-term viability (the strongest single predictor of whether the channel compounds or flatlines). If these three are healthy by Day 30, AUDM has earned algorithmic distribution and ad revenue follows. If any one of them breaches its floor by Day 60, run the matching pivot trigger from § 4 — don't wait for Day 90 to act.
